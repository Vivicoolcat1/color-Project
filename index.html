Color Match

by: Vianne Schwaebe

This game was made for my level 2 project at The League of Amazing Programmers.
Click here to download and play!

1. Why I Made This Game

I made this game because a version of this game used to be my favorite game. The idea of such a simple but complicated game inspired me to make my own take on this game.

2. What I Learned

I learned you can always have suggestions to make a game better and it will never be truly done. Some students told me too change the speeds in my game and to change the colors after every bar.

3. Architecture of My Game

The parts of my game include a ball(you), a camera , a finish line, 5 bar pieces in a bar( the different colored rectangles), and a total of 10 bars in a game. If the ball is the same color as the bar piece it goes through, if its not the same color and it still goes through then the ball (you)dies. The camera moves the screen up every time the ball gets to a certain point. When the ball crosses through the finish line it ends the game and you win.

4. Technical Implementation

To have a camera meant to be able to see a window of the game at a time rather than see the whole game at once. I am proud of this aspect because it was way harder to do than to show the whole game at once.

5. Most Challenging Part

The camera was one very hard technical part of my game . To set up the camera I had to start the ball at a negative value. Then I had to establish the minimum Y value and push up the screen when the ball hit that value. The difficulties of this were to figure out at what point to push the screen up and how to push it up. I decided to move the screen up when the ball reached near the top of the window. To move the screen up I tested different algorithms until one pushed up the screen the right amount.

6. Future Fixes and Updates

I would make the ball a different color after every bar , change the speeds of each bar and maybe add some music to the game to make it more entertaining.
